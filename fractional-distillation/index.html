Why does chain length matter?

The pattern: longer chains have stronger forces between the molecules. More energy is needed to pull them apart, so the boiling point is higher. Long chains also tangle together, making the liquid thicker and harder to ignite.

Key words

hydrocarbon
A molecule made of hydrogen and carbon atoms only.
fraction
A group of hydrocarbons with a similar number of carbon atoms and similar boiling points.
evaporate
To turn from a liquid into a gas (vapour).
condense
To turn from a gas back into a liquid.
boiling point
The temperature at which a liquid boils and becomes a gas.
temperature gradient
A steady change in temperature — here, hot at the bottom of the column and cool at the top.
viscous
Thick and slow to pour. Honey is more viscous than water.
flammable
Catches fire easily.
